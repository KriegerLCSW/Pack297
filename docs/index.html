WELCOME TO CUB SCOUT PACK 297!

Welcome to Pack 297! We welcome boys and girls in Kindergarten through fifth grade. We go camping, race cars, hike, explore nature, play games, and serve our community. Pack meetings are on the 4th Friday each month; den meetings are every other week. All meetings are at St. Hubert Church, 729 Grand Canyon St, Hoffman Estates, IL 60169. Click HERE for a map!

Why Join?

  • Camping, hiking, and racing cars!
  • Family events all year round
  • Make friends and learn new skills
  • How do I get my son or daughter started?

Call or text George Krieger, at (847) 989-5056, or email at kriegerlcsw@gmail.com. OR JUST CLICK HERE to register at the Scouting America website.

  • How much does it cost to join?

Dues are $165 per year. Of that total, $100 goes to BSA, Inc., our parent organization, and $65 goes to Pack 297. Parents will also need to purchase a uniform for their Cub Scout.

  • Are parents and siblings allowed to come to scout events?

Not only are parents and siblings allowed to come, they are encouraged to come! Cub Scouts is a family organization, and we love having family involved!

  • My daughter is already in Girl Scouts. Can she be a Cub Scout too?
    Absolutely! There aren't any other activities that exclude a girl (or boy) from being a Cub Scout!
  • Do I have to join your Pack (Pack 297)?
    Not at all! Your child is able to join any Cub Scout Pack in the world, but ours is probably closer! Seriously, there are at least three other Cub Scout groups (called Packs) in Schaumburg Township.
  • Are there events all year round, or just during the school year?
    We meet regularly during the year.  Over the summer, we do not have regular den or pack meetings, but we do have special events during the summer, such as swim events at Seascape Family Aquatic Center, marching in the Hoffman Estates Fourth of July Parade, and camping out overnight after the Schaumburg Boomers game on the field!
    Once school starts for the new school year, we will return to regular den and pack meetings. Most dens meet once every two weeks, and the pack meets as a whole every month.
  • What happens after 5th grade?
    In February of 5th grade, the Cub Scouts “Crossover” to Scouts BSA. They used to be called “Boy Scouts”. At that point, they have graduated from Cub Scouts!
  • How can the parents of Cub Scouts get more involved?

Cub Scouts depends on parent involvement to keep the pack running.  We are always looking for eager parents to be Den Leaders and Assistant Den Leaders,  and no experience is necessary!

We also have a variety of other leader positions, such as Committee Chair, Treasurer, New Member Coordinator, and Advancement Coordinator.
