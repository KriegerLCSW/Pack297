Welcome to Pack 297!

Welcome to Pack 297! We welcome boys and girls in Kindergarten through fifth grade. We go camping, race cars, hike, explore nature, play games, and serve our community. Pack meetings are on the 4th Friday each month; den meetings are every other week. All meetings are at St. Hubert Church, 729 Grand Canyon St, Hoffman Estates, IL 60169. Call, text, or email, and someone will respond within 48 hours!

Why Join?

  • Camping, hiking, and racing cars!
  • Family events all year round
  • Make friends and learn new skills

Pack 297 FAQ 2025 (Version 1.6, 06/17/2025)

  1. How do I get my son or daughter started?

Just scan the QR code with your phone’s camera and follow the link, or call the Cubmaster, George Krieger, at (847) 989-5056.

  1. How much does it cost to join?

Dues are $165 per year.  Of that total, $100 goes to BSA, Inc., our parent organization, and $65 goes to Pack 297.  Parents will also need to purchase a uniform for their Cub Scout.

  1. Are parents and siblings allowed to come to scout events?

Not only are parents and siblings allowed to come, they are encouraged to come!  Cub Scouts is a family organization, and we love having family involved!

DENS usually meet every other week.  All of the dens together are called a PACK.  The Pack meets monthly.  Those meetings take place at St. Hubert Church, 729 Grand Canyon St, Hoffman Estates, IL 60169

  1. Are there events all year round, or just during the school year?

We meet regularly during the year.  Over the summer, we have special events during the summer, such as swim events at Seascape Family Aquatic Center, marching in the Hoffman Estates Fourth of July Parade, and camping out overnight after the Schaumburg Boomers game on the field!

  1. Do you go camping? How often?

We go camping overnight at least two times during the year, once at our annual Crossover event, and once in August after the Schaumburg Boomers game!  We also build race cars, go hiking, learn about nature, play games, and participate in community service projects!

  1.  How can the parents of Cub Scouts get more involved?

Cub Scouts depends on parent involvement to keep the pack running.  We are always looking for eager parents to be Den Leaders and Assistant Den Leaders, and no experience is necessary!
